133. Patient on long-term bisphosphonates → extraction → antibiotics?  
     1. Pre-op only  
     2. Pre \+ during healing  
     3. Not needed ✅  
* \* Stopping bisphosphonates prior to invasive dental procedures is not recommended as there is no evidence it reduces the risk of osteonecrosis of the jaw (ONJ).  
* \*Some clinicians may choose to postpone IV bisphosphonate infusions until after a planned procedure, but this should be discussed with the treating physician.  
* \*Routine prophylactic antibiotics are not necessary unless there are local or systemic infection risk factors.  
* \*No evidence supports the use of antibiotics to reduce ONJ risk after invasive dental procedures.
